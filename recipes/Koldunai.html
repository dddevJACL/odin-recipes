Koldunai

Description

Traditional Lithuanian dumplings, usually served with a meat filling, sour cream, and fried onions.

Ingredients

  • 6 cups flour
  • 1 cup butter or margarine
  • 2 eggs, slightly beaten
  • 3 tablespoons of sour cream
  • 1 and a quarter cups of water
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 pound ground meat
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 2 tablespoons bread crumbs, salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, cut butter into flour until crumbly
  2. Make a well in the centre of the flour mixture, and add beaten eggs, sour cream, salt and water into the well.
  3. Mix
  4. Turn out onto a floured board and knead dough until it is firm and well-mixed.
  5. Let dough rest for several hours, preferably overnight.
  6. Mix the ground meat, onion, egg, the 2 tablespoons of breadcrumbs, salt and pepper to make the meat filling.
  7. Divide dough in quarters
  8. Turn dough onto floured board and roll out as thinly as possible.
  9. Cut out 3 inch rounds with biscuit cutter or the rim of a drinking glass
  10. Place a spoonful of filling in the centre of each round.
  11. Fold the dough over the meat and pinch the edges together, making sure they are well sealed.
  12. Drop the Koldunai into boiling salted water
  13. Boil for 5 to 10 minutes
  14. Serve with sour cream, fried bacon and fried onions.
